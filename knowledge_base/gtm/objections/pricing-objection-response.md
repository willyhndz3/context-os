# Pricing Objection Response

When prospects say "it's too expensive" or push back on pricing.

## Framework: Reframe Before Respond

Don't defend the price. Reframe the conversation around value.

### Step 1: Acknowledge
> "I hear you - budget is always a consideration."

### Step 2: Discovery Question
> "Help me understand - what are you comparing us to?"
> "What's the cost of not solving this problem?"

### Step 3: Reframe Value
- Focus on ROI, not cost
- Use their numbers from discovery
- Calculate time saved × hourly rate

### Step 4: Flexibility (if needed)
- Payment terms (annual vs monthly)
- Phased rollout
- Pilot program

## Common Scenarios

### "Competitor X is cheaper"
Response: "They often are at first glance. What's included in that price? Let's compare apples to apples - implementation, support, integrations..."

### "It's not in the budget"
Response: "Understood. When do budget decisions get made? And who else would need to be involved to evaluate this properly?"

### "Can you do better on price?"
Response: "I can look at options. But first - if price weren't a factor, is this the solution you'd want?"
